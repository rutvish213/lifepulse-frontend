Frequently Asked Questions
Who can donate blood?
Anyone between 18–65 years of age, weighing at least 50 kg, and in good health can donate blood. You must wait at least 56 days between whole blood donations. People with certain medical conditions or on specific medications may be deferred.
How long does donation take?
The actual blood donation takes only 8–10 minutes. However, including registration, mini health check-up, donation, and refreshment break, the entire process takes about 45–60 minutes.
Is blood donation safe?
Yes, absolutely. All Life Pulse partner blood banks use sterile, single-use needles. The process is completely safe. Donors may feel mild fatigue or dizziness which passes quickly. Our trained staff monitor you throughout.
How quickly can I get blood in an emergency?
For critical emergencies, our SOS system dispatches alerts to nearby donors and hospitals within 60 seconds. The average response time for critical requests is under 3 minutes. For routine requests, we match within 2–4 hours.
Is Life Pulse free to use?
Yes! Life Pulse is completely free for all donors, patients, and their families. We are a non-profit initiative funded by government grants and CSR contributions from our hospital partners.
How is my data kept secure?
We use AES-256 encryption for all stored data, JWT authentication for API access, and OTP verification for all critical actions. We are HIPAA compliant and never share personal data with third parties without explicit consent.
